Portfolio Overview

A Single Transferable Property Right

The intellectual property is held by Regulator AI Global, Inc., a Delaware C-corporation with a single shareholder. It is available for structured licensing or strategic acquisition by qualified counterparties operating in regulated or high-stakes environments.

The portfolio is built around the Cognitive Transport Protocol™ (CTP) and a set of governance-aware architectures for evaluating and supervising complex AI-driven systems. It is protected by seven U.S. provisional patent filings submitted in March 2026, designed as an interlocking defensive perimeter around the core intellectual property.

The architecture aligns with emerging governance and risk frameworks including EU AI Act Article 14, NIST AI RMF, DORA, ISO 42001, and sectoral standards such as Basel and NERC — positioning the portfolio as an infrastructure layer for AI oversight in regulated environments.

The IP may be incorporated into a range of commercial offerings, including governance and compliance infrastructure, risk-assessment and underwriting tools, evaluation frameworks for AI-heavy mergers and acquisitions, and certification or regulatory oversight platforms.

Patent Portfolio

Seven Filings. One Defensive Perimeter.

The seven provisional filings collectively address an interlocking set of problem domains. Each filing reinforces the others, creating a defensive perimeter around the core intellectual property rather than a single isolated claim.

01

Discovery of hidden interaction liabilities in autonomous and AI-driven systems — identifying exposure that is not visible through conventional diligence or operational review.

02

Quantification of interaction risk, supervisory capacity, and governance maturity — producing measurable assessments rather than qualitative opinions.

03

Deterministic containment of AI behavior under bounded supervisory resources — ensuring oversight remains operative when human capacity is constrained.

04

Integration airlocks governing cross-system interaction and escalation pathways — controlling how AI systems connect, interact, and escalate across organizational or infrastructural boundaries.

05

Governance telemetry and evidentiary traces for regulators, insurers, and counsel — producing documentation that is generated by the system rather than reconstructed after the fact.

06

Predictive simulation of cascade failures across complex software and infrastructure estates — modeling how failure propagates before it occurs, not after.

07

Supervisory continuity across transaction and integration boundaries — ensuring that oversight is maintained through the moments of maximum liability transfer in M&A and system convergence events.

Transaction Structure

Structured Licensing or Strategic Acquisition

The intellectual property may be incorporated into a range of commercial offerings. Regulator AI Global is seeking counterparties interested in licensing or acquiring the portfolio as a transferable intellectual property asset.

Option 01
Licensing

Exclusive or non-exclusive licensing by industry vertical, use case, or geography. The licensee incorporates the IP into their own offerings while Regulator AI Global retains the underlying property rights. Structures may be tailored by scope, duration, and field of use.

Option 02
Strategic Acquisition

Full acquisition of the portfolio and supporting documentation — patents, trademark, codebase, technical specifications, and white papers — as a transferable intellectual property asset. The acquirer assumes ownership of all rights and supporting materials.

Option 03
Embedded Partnership

Partnership structures in which the framework is embedded into the buyer's offerings under the buyer's brand. The IP infrastructure operates as a foundational layer within the partner's product or service without requiring public attribution to Regulator AI Global.
